Methodology

What is Walk-Forward Backtesting?

Most "ML trading signals" are trained on a full historical dataset and then tested on that same data. That's called overfitting — the model memorizes the past rather than learning to predict the future. A backtest on training data will always look great, even for a completely useless model.

Walk-forward backtesting is different. The dataset is split into multiple sequential windows. For each window, the model is trained on everything before the test period, then evaluated on the test period it never saw. Repeat 5 times across the full history. The resulting directional accuracy is a genuine out-of-sample measurement.

Step 1
Historical Split
Up to 10 years of daily price data per ticker is divided into 5 walk-forward windows, each roughly 80 trading days (~400 total test samples).
Step 2
Blind Training
For each window, the model trains on data from before the test period only. It has no access to the prices it's about to predict.
Step 3
Directional Accuracy
The model predicts UP or DOWN. Each prediction is checked against actual prices. Accuracy across all 5 windows is the displayed DA%.

The Models

Four Models, One Consensus

Every signal is backed by four independent ML models that each predict the direction separately. When they agree, conviction is higher. When they diverge, the signal is rated Weak regardless of the raw accuracy number.

📈
Linear Regression
The simplest baseline — useful for seeing whether a ticker has any detectable linear trend. Low weight in conviction scoring; mainly serves as a reality check against the more complex models.
🌲
Random Forest
An ensemble of decision trees that captures non-linear interactions between indicators. Uses OOB (out-of-bag) scoring for an additional internal accuracy check, with no extra train/test split needed.
⚡
LightGBM
Gradient-boosted trees — the primary model used by the live paper-trading bot. Handles high-dimensional indicator data efficiently and tends to have the highest walk-forward directional accuracy across most tickers.
🎯
Adaptive LGB
LightGBM with per-ticker feature selection using Mutual Information scoring. Different stocks respond to different signals — NVDA may be momentum-driven while utilities are mean-reverting. The adaptive model drops the 10 weakest features for each ticker before training.
